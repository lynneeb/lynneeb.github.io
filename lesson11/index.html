Freezing Temperatures Across Idaho

By Lynnette Beatty

Brace yourself for a cold winter! According to the Idaho Farm Bureau Federation, the current cold spell could hurt local crops. Sweet corn, potatoes, dry beans, and onions are at risk of freezing. The National Weather Service reports that the Northern Panhandle and Central Idaho received some light snowfall already this month. Eastern Idaho got several inches of snow. Record-low temperatures are expected across the state.

A National Weather Service meteorologist said the high winds, low temperatures and snow are caused by a low pressure system from Canada. Most regions across Idaho are going to experience record low temperatures. The record low in Boise, set back in 1985, was 26 degrees in October. The weather forecast expects to break that record by Thursday morning.

Reference: KTVB Local Weather
