Soda Springs, Idaho

by Lynnette Beatty

The city of Soda Springs is located in the Caribou County of Idaho. In the 2010 census, it had a population of 3,058. Since the county was established in the 1860s, Soda Springs has been the county seat. The city received its name from hundreds of natural springs made of carbonated water that are found in the city and around the area. The springs were a famous landmark on the Oregon Trail in the 19th century and were well known to the Native Americans.

In 1934, the founding fathers of the city drilled into a chamber of highly pressurized carbon dioxide gas and cold water, looking for hot water to create a “hot pool” bathing attraction. A geyser was released and flooded the downtown area, until it was manually capped weeks later. The geyser is now released as a tourist attraction every hour.
