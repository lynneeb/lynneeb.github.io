Fish Haven, Idaho

by Lynnette Beatty

Fish Haven is located in Bear Lake County of Idaho. It has an elevation of 5,924 ft. A Fish Haven post office was established in 1867 and was operated until 1962. It is an unincorporated community that resides along the shores of Bear Lake, 2.5 miles north of the Utah border. Fish Haven was first settled in 1864. It received its name due to the fact that Bear Lake is a popular fishing spot.

A bike trail exists around Bear Lake that offers a spectacular view of the lake and surrounding area. The mountain forest is particularly beautiful in autumn. The Bear Lake National Wildlife Preserve has been established at the north end of the lake. It is a popular tourist attraction for bird watching.
